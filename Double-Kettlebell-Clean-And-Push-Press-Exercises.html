Double Kettlebell Exercises -Kettlebell Clean And Push Press

The Double Kettlebell Clean and Push Press is a fantastic alternative to the Double Kettlebell Clean and Jerk if shoulder and/or thoracic mobility is a problem for you. In fact, it might be even be a BETTER option due to the "half press" discovered after the leg drive throughout the arm lockout overhead.

Perform them for low to medium reps for power and strength advancement along with total muscle development. Or perform them with higher reps to turbo-charge your conditioning levels.

1. Start with the kettlebells in front of you.

2. Strongly trek the kettlebells behind you beneath your hips.

3. Tuck your elbows into your sides and roll the kettlebells around your wrists up the front of your body.

4. Keep the kettlebells pulled close to the body.

5. Gently put them in the rack position. Keep the elbows glued to your ribs.

6. Keeping the upper body vertical (perpendicular to the ground), dip your knees quickly and after that align them.

7. At the instant they straighten, use the force produced from your legs to drive the kettlebells overhead locking out the elbows.

8. To reduce them, either pull them pull back to the rack or let them "freefall" soaking up or capturing them by dipping your knees.

9. Align your knees and set up for another Clean.

10. Re-clean the kettlebells and carry out actions 6 through 9 again for the Push Press.

11. Perform as many associates as you wish.

12. Location the kettlebells on the ground the same way you would the Double Kettlebell Swing.
