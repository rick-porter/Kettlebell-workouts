Double Kettlebell Clean and Jerk Exercise with Geoff Neupert

The Double Kettlebell Clean and Jerk (C+J) is an exercise of legend. Rumor has it, that the Spetsnaz, the Soviet/Russian Special Forces systems, performed their PT specifically with high repetition C+J's using a set of 24kg kettlebells. Multiple sets of 20 to 30 associates were not unusual.

Later, Russian Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu clubs instituted high associate C+J's as part of their upper level brown and black belt promotion tests.

If you were going to spend your time doing "one" kettlebell workout, this very well could be it.

How To Double Kettlebell Clean and Jerk

1. Start with the kettlebells in front of you.

2. Aggressively hike the kettlebells behind you underneath your hips.

3. Tuck your elbows into your sides and roll the kettlebells around your wrists up the front of your body.

4. Keep the kettlebells pulled close to the body.

5. Gently position them in the rack position. Keep the elbows glued to your ribs.

6. Similar to the Double Kettlebell Push Press, keeping the torso vertical

(perpendicular to the ground), dip your knees quickly and after that correct them.

7. As the kettlebells pass your forehead, drop below the kettlebells ending in a "mini-squat" with your arms locked overhead.

8. Straighten your knees and stand.

9. To lower the kettlebells, either pull them pull back to the rack or let them "freefall" soaking up or capturing them by dipping your knees.

10. To carry out another representative, repeat steps 6 through 9.

11. Lower the kettlebells to the ground the same way you would the Double Swing.
