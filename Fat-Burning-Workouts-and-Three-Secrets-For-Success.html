Fat Burning Workouts and Three Secrets For Success

Step into any gym or health club in the world and I can guarantee you a 80% chance that any given person in there is trying to achieve a fat burning exercises.

However what is a fat burning exercise?

What does one really look like and how do you understand if you're really performing fat burning workouts or just killing time?

Here are 3 trademarks of weight loss exercises:

1. They require your body to utilize all of its muscles.

Notification how I utilized the word "muscles" and said nothing about the word "cardio." See, that's the very first mistake people all over the world are making - they center their exercises around cardio.

Cardio burns calories, but in many cases, it simply burns calories while you're performing it. Not only that, however "cardio only" workouts tend to strip your body of its muscle.

There's that word once again: Muscle.

What's the big deal about that?

Muscle is your "fat loss equipment." It's where fat is burned. Muscle is what triggers your body to use energy, more energy. The more muscle you have on your body, the more energy your body will need and the more energy you will burn.

So, your fat loss exercises need to be structured around training your muscles - resistance/strength training. And you need to use large movements that need a lot of muscles to work at one time. These are exercises like squats, deadlifts, push-ups, rows, chin ups, and so on

2. They cause you to breathe actually difficult and make you sweat a lot.

Ok, I'm sorry if I offend you with what I'm about to state, however here it goes: Walking on the treadmill (while hanging on) watching Oprah while simultaneously talking to your buddy on the next treadmill, is not a weight loss exercise. It is, in weight loss terms, counterproductive and a waste of your time.

If you actually want to burn fat, crank up the incline of the treadmill and the speed to simply listed below the point where you definitely have to run, and do that for 60 seconds. When the 60 seconds is up, slow the treadmill down and drop the incline back to normal (2.0 is normal).

What will happen is that you'll be out of breath. You'll begin sweating. A lot. Now repeat that another 4 to nine times and after that perform a five minute cool down. You will still be breathing difficult and probably sweating a lot.

That's a fat loss exercise.

3. They offer you a good "warm" feeling for several hours afterward.

Regardless of weather condition or not you pick to perform a workout like I pointed out in the first trademark or the 2nd, or a mix of the two (actually the very best fat loss exercise), you'll understand you achieved success if you are still sweating an hour or 2 later or feel warm all over. That is a sign that you elevated your metabolic process.

Congratulations! Your body remains in the process of burning fat! And this means you are on your method to accomplishing your weight loss objectives.

If your fat loss workouts don't consist of these three trademarks, they're just not fat burning. Unless you're content in losing your time, make the necessary modifications so you can burn as much fat as possible in as little time as possible.
