Kettlebell Complexes are Your Ticket to a Stronger Lower Back

If you've ever suffered from lower back pain, you know that it can be a real pain in the butt.

If you've ever suffered from truly bad lower pain in the back, like I have, you know it can be incapacitating. Even the most ordinary daily tasks like strolling or standing from a chair can be a challenge.

Sometimes, lower pain in the back and injury happens due to a variety of aspects, however a few of the significant culprits are muscle weak points and muscle imbalances.

Kettlebells can help fix those imbalances and weaknesses. Prior to we look at how kettlebells can assist, lets take a look at a few of the typical weak points and imbalances.

1. Weak Abdominals. Your abs, when working properly help keep your hips from moving too much or moving into the incorrect positions when moving, be it regular moving or exercising.

2. Tight Hip Flexors. Your hip flexors allow you to walk, run, climb up, stand, sit, lunge, twist, and turn. When these muscles get tight, they pull the hips out of its natural alignment. If your abs are weak, this ends up being a major issue. Also, it is understood that tight hip flexors avoid the abdominals from working properly.

3. Weak Butt. Yes, the ol' derriere is created to help you move forward and offer a counterbalance when flexing. When this muscle is weak in combination with the others, it can spell catastrophe for your lower back.

4. Weak Hamstrings. These are the muscles that run down the back of your legs. They likewise are developed, together with the butt, to move you forward and offer a counterbalance to your upper body when flexing.

There are obviously other muscles that can add to back pain, but these are some of the primary offenders.

Fundamental kettlebell exercises such as the Deadlift and the Swing strengthen your weak areas - your butt, abdominals, and hamstrings. These kettlebell exercises aid in fixing these muscular imbalances safeguarding your lower back throughout time.

Honestly, most of the kettlebell exercises concentrate on the hips and core, and when performed correctly, actually have a magical impact on the body. Not only do tight hips from years of sitting chill out and become more powerful and more durable, however it's not uncommon for old shoulder injuries to vanish as overly-mobile shoulders become more steady.
