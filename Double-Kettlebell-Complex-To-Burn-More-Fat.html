The Strong! Program

There are three different training cycles within the "Strong!" program.

The very first is an 8 to 12-week block that trains your strength, based upon your 4 repeating maximum (RM), keeping the number of repeatings low-- in between 1 and 3-- and the number of sets high. Rest between sets is "... as necessary ..." with the objective being "... to make each and every rep." Auto-regulation at its heart.

Sounds strikingly comparable to EMOM A+A (every minute on the minute, alactic + aerobic) procedures from Strong Endurance ™, doesn't it?

The second cycle trains work capacity and/or hypertrophy. It has two variations: the "Short Course" and the "Slow and Steady." The very first variation has you double your starting volume in 4 to 6 weeks. The 2nd, is more merciful, and spreads it out over 16 to 24 weeks.

Both are developed to make your old 4RM beginning weight seem like a toy.

Nevertheless, since its goal is to build muscle mass and/or construct your capability to do and recuperate from work, there's a more stringent rest between sets-- 1 to 2 minutes. The principle is "to get a pump with a heavy weight."

This does start to get mildly glycolytic, however if you choose the "Slow and Steady," it is not intolerably so. Your body adapts very well, and those who stay with it are rewarded with the "Holy Grail" of strength training-- more muscle mass, increased strength levels, and (generally) lower body fat levels-- though this will be highly affected by dietary choices. This is accomplished in around ninety minutes each week, no matter age or training experience.

The "Brief Course" is more severe, ruthless, requiring, and absolutely for those with exceptional recovery abilities and less requiring schedules. (I do NOT suggest this for parents of little kids, or any kids truly due to the fact that you should have the ability to get A Great Deal Of sleep.) Most of folks choose the "Slow and Steady" for this reason.

The third and last stage is a 5 to 8-week program that profits from all the work you've done to date, and is focused on weight loss. It's a density-based program-- you will do the exact same quantity of work in less time-- which actually means more total work. To do this, you'll lower your pause.

Does lactic acid build up? Definitely. Which's for an extremely specific purpose: growth hormonal agent, your most effective fat burning hormonal agent, is released in the presence of lactic acid.

Do you require to do this last phase? Never.

In fact, most people stop after the "Slow and Steady" and start over, using heavier kettlebells, with their leaner, more muscular bodies.

The "One" Program

The "One" program is a various beast entirely. It's a conditioning program implied to be performed utilizing the Double Swing. This is because the Double Swing is very low-skill compared to the Double Clean and Press, and has a much shorter stroke, so less can fail. Both of these characteristics-- low skill and short stroke-- indicate there is less focus needed. Perfect for a conditioning exercise.

It begins mildly "quick glycolytic" and goes full-blown glycolytic rapidly. It is definitely an obstacle ... but not necessarily “fun.”
