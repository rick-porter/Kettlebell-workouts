One of the top New Year's resolutions every year is to "lose weight." This I believe is the single biggest factor leading to weight loss failure.

Weight loss is a "catch all" term. The word "weight" refers to the force of the Earth's gravitational field on the mass of your body. So what you actually indicate when you state you wish to "drop weight" is that you wish to "lose mass."

But, the trick to achieving any goal is to be as specific as possible. So you want to reduce weight - lose mass - however simply any mass?

Sure you want to see the number on the scale go down, however don't you wish to lose the best mass? What if you still have a gut or saddlebags but you're 30 pounds lighter than you are now? Were you as effective as you wished to be?

Most likely not.

What you really wished to do without verbalizing it was to "lose fat." Fat, is what gives you a few of those unsightly shapes that drive you crazy when you look in the mirror - the lovehandles, the "pooch," the gut, or the saddlebags.
